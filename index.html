Why MARTA Faces These Challenges

MARTAâ€™s limited coverage is rooted in historical and socio-economic decisions that prioritized car-centric development over public transportation.

Atlantaâ€™s urban planning favored highways and suburban sprawl, limiting the ability to expand rail systems.


MARTAâ€™s funding constraints and inconsistent political support have historically hindered expansion efforts. Current funding efforts revolve around solely expanding rapid bus transit, which does not directly address congestion and related issues that rail-based transit does.


Comparisons with cities like Seattle, which have embraced organized, combined rail and bus transit-oriented growth via its Sound Transit, show what Atlanta could achieve with similar efforts. Atlanta would benefit from an approach that emphasizes expansion, access, and efficient urban planning.

Expanding and Optimizing MARTA

A strategic, focused plan to expand MARTAâ€™s bus and rail systems, informed by data and community feedback, can make public transit a viable option for all.

The plan comprises of research to develop a proposal to MARTA that identifies the following additions to the current transportation system:

  • New bus routes with shorter distances between stops, along with estimated schedules. This improves coverage of underserved neighborhoods. Factors like travel time, frequency, and latency will be crucial in establishing opportunities for new routes that boost ridership and access.

  • Addition of new stations and new lines to connect high-demand areas. This will incorporate data analysis on current and projected areas of high population density and how strategic, increased transit access can spur sustainable urban growth. Environmental costs will also be considered to design the best solution moving forward.

In identifying these expansion opportunities, in addition to geospatial data analysis and feasibility studies, community outreach will ensure that the expansion aligns with commuter needs and garners large public support. This will involve surveys to analyze feedback on current transportation issues and popular desired improvements.

Better Bus System

By adopting an organized plan on frequency, route expansion, and technology, Atlanta can create a more efficient and reliable public transportation system. Issues like traffic congestion and delays would be minimized, enhancing commuter experience for all. Sustainable urban expansion would be promoted as well. Environmental benefits would also be realized.
